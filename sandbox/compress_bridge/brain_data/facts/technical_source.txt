Q: What is TerraLens? A: TerraLens is a hierarchical, C++-accelerated optimization engine designed for high-dimensional non-convex loss landscapes.
Q: How does TerraLens treat optimization? A: TerraLens conceptualizes weight space as physical terrain and treats optimization as a topographic survey.
Q: What are the four layers of the TerraLens engine? A: The layers are Satellite Scanner, GPS Grid, Radar Probe, and Skip Engine.
Q: What is the Satellite Scanner? A: Layer 1 is the Satellite Scanner, which uses sparse Monte Carlo sampling to prune high-loss regions.
Q: What is the GPS Grid? A: Layer 2 is the GPS Grid, which uses coordinate-wise decomposition for O(N) scaling.
Q: What is the Radar Probe? A: Layer 3 is the Radar Probe, which uses C++ Hessian diagonal sensing to identify mountains versus valleys.
Q: What is the Skip Engine? A: Layer 4 is the Skip Engine, which uses curvature-triggered jumps to bypass non-convex barriers.
